Second Griffin

Like many personal projects of mine, Second Griffin Tables and Results grew out of a need that I saw, combined with a desire to try out Google's App Engine. Unfortunately, the idea for a score reporting website never took off, but building it was a good expereince for me and a way to keep current on web development.

Plus Nine Scores

Sick of never having a scorepad while playing cards, I developed Plus Nine as an Android app so that my friends and I could always keep track of who is the better Hearts player. (Unfortunately, it's not me.) Trying to get some exposure on social networks, I also built a website so that players could share their scores on Facebook and Twitter. ReportNet

When our old reporting website just wasn't cutting it anymore, my boss called asked me to design a new version. Designed and built in Microsoft's Silverlight, the website has drawn interest from our current clients and has potential to add value for new clients.

Weather Graph

There are hundreds of weather apps on the Android Market, so why write another one? Because none of them did exactly what I want, which is to show an hourly view of future weather conditions. Maybe it's the mathematician in me that likes to see graphs, or the developer who likes to try something new, but using only web technologies—especially <canvas>—to build this app was a gratifying, if ever-so-slightly profitable, project.
