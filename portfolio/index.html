Second Griffin

Like many personal projects of mine, Second Griffin Tables and Results grew out of a need that I saw, combined with a desire to learn something new. The need was a decent league score reporting website; the someething new was Python on Google's App Engine. Unfortunately, the idea never took off, but building it was a good experience for me and a way to keep current on web development.

Plus Nine Scores

Sick of never having a pen and paper while playing cards, I developed Plus Nine as an Android app so that my friends and I could always keep track of who is the better Hearts player. (Unfortunately, it's not me.) Trying to get some exposure on social networks, I also built a website so that players could share their scores on Facebook and Twitter. ReportNet

When our old reporting website just wasn't cutting it anymore, my boss asked me to design a new version. Designed and built in Microsoft's Silverlight and ASP.NET, the website has drawn interest from our current clients and has potential to add value for new clients.

Weather Graph

There are hundreds of weather apps on the Android Market, so why write another one? Because none of them did exactly what I want, which is to show an hourly view of future weather conditions. Maybe it's the mathematician in me that likes to see graphs, or the developer who likes to try something new, but using only web technologies—especially <canvas> and javascript—to build this app was a gratifying, if ever-so-slightly profitable, project.
