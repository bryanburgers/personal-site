Second Griffin

Like many personal projects of mine, Second Griffin Tables and Results grew out of a need that I saw, combined with a desire to learn something new. The need was a decent league score reporting website; the something new, Python on Google's App Engine. Unfortunately, the idea never took off, but building it was a good experience for me and a way to keep current on web development.

Plus Nine Scores

Sick of never having a pen and paper while playing cards, I developed Plus Nine as an Android app so that my friends and I could always keep track of who is the better Hearts player. (Unfortunately, it's not me.) Trying to get some exposure for the app on social networks, I also built a website so that players could share their scores on Facebook and Twitter. For example, here's one of a game of mini golf with the family.

Premier Networking

If converting Photoshop compositions to working websites is a web developer's bread and butter, the freelance project I did for Premier Networking put the bacon on the table. While working for Brand New Graphics, I took this design from their designer and created an attractive, semantically marked-up website.

Weather Graph

There are hundreds of weather apps on the Android Market, so why write another one? Because none of them did exactly what I want: show an hourly view of future weather conditions. Maybe it's the mathematician in me that likes to see graphs, or the developer who likes to try something new, but using only web technologies—especially <canvas> and javascript—to build this app resulted in a product that could be easily ported to another mobile platform.

ReportNet

When our old reporting website at Interstates just wasn't cutting it anymore and we needed something more interactive and responsive, my boss asked me to design a new version. Designed and built in Microsoft's Silverlight and ASP.NET, (Interstates is, in fact, a Microsoft shop) the website has drawn interest from our current clients and has potential to add value for new clients.
